About Me

I began my post-college career at an Atlanta tech startup where I was focused on operations and documenting technical processes. Due to this need to convert tribal knowledge to paper, I had to dig deep into Engineering's workflow and began delving into techincal jargon. Here, I began to see the endless possibilities that coding and database experience can offer a young, malleable mind.

As my career has progressed, I've been eager to develop my technical and coding prowess even more. I aspire to be a well-rounded resource that my clients and coworkers can rely on and collaborate with. Through the Georgia Tech Coding Bootcamp, I have been exposed to frontend frameworks such as HTML, CSS, and Materialize and Bootstrap CSS, as well as backend technologies including APIs, JQuery, Javascript, Node.js, and Firebase.

Code is like the DNA of web design, and I've been extremely fortunate to delve into a community of web Watsons and Cricks. Employer support and my continued education has been key to my success, and I look forward to continuing my web development education and contributing to a lifelong portfolio that reflects my growing skill set.
